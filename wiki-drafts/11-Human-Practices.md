## 1. Overview: Why Human Practices Matters / 概述：人类实践为何重要

**English:**

In synthetic biology, the line between "what we can build" and "what we should build" is drawn not in the laboratory — it is drawn through conversation. Human Practices is the bridge between our engineered cells and the world they are designed to enter. For friskoli, a project aimed at a domain as demanding as space, Human Practices is not a supplementary activity. It is a compass.

From the beginning, we recognized that our project could not be developed in isolation. The challenges of microgravity bioreactor operation are real, complex, and still poorly understood outside specialist circles. Understanding these challenges — from the people who face them daily — was essential to designing something that could genuinely contribute. This page documents that journey: the doubts that nearly derailed us, the conversations that refocused us, and the ethical reasoning that keeps us grounded.

**中文：**

在合成生物学中，"我们能够构建什么"与"我们应该构建什么"之间的界限不是在实验室中划定的——它是在对话中形成的。人类实践是我们工程化细胞与它们将要进入的世界之间的桥梁。对于 friskoli——一个瞄准太空这一苛刻领域的项目——人类实践不是辅助活动，而是指南针。

从一开始我们就认识到，这个项目不能孤立地开发。微重力生物反应器运行的挑战是真实的、复杂的，在专业圈之外仍然鲜为人知。理解这些挑战——从每天面对它们的人那里去理解——对于设计出真正有贡献的东西至关重要。本页面记录了这段旅程：那些几乎使我们偏离方向的怀疑，那些使我们重新聚焦的对话，以及使我们脚踏实地的伦理思考。

## 2. The Origin Story — From Aspiration to Doubt / 起源故事——从憧憬到怀疑

**English:**

### 2.1 A First-Year Team Chooses Space

We are a first-year iGEM team. When the time came to select a track, the choice of Space Track was almost reflexive. Our institution — Shenzhen University and the Shenzhen Institute of Advanced Technology — sits at the intersection of academic biology and advanced engineering. Space research, astrobiology, and life-support technology are part of the institutional conversation. Choosing the Space Track felt natural. Ambitious, yes. But natural.

At first, the excitement was pure. We imagined bacteria floating in orbital laboratories. We imagined our engineered cells contributing to a future where humans live and work beyond Earth. The narrative was clean: synthetic biology enables space exploration. We wanted to be part of that story.

### 2.2 The Hidden Doubt

But as weeks passed and we began the real work of literature review and project scoping, a quiet unease settled in. We explored dozens of potential directions:

- **Resource recycling in closed-loop habitats** — a well-trodden path with many prior efforts.
- **Algae-bacteria co-culture for oxygen and nutrient exchange** — elegant in theory, extremely difficult to control in practice.
- **Space food production via engineered microorganisms** — a crucial need, but the project felt disconnected from our molecular biology strengths.
- **Bioreactor optimization for microgravity** — this one kept resurfacing, but each time we circled it, we found more questions than answers.

Each brainstorming session left us a little less certain. The ideas were interesting, but something felt missing. The harder we pushed, the more we realized: we didn't actually understand what the *real problems* in space bioprocessing were. We were reading papers. We were not talking to people. We were making assumptions.

### 2.3 The Almost-Abandonment

There came a point — it lasted about two weeks — where the team seriously considered abandoning the Space Track altogether. The doubts had crystallized into a single, uncomfortable question that nobody wanted to voice:

*"We've never truly set foot in space. Would a space bioreactor actually work like one on Earth?"*

This question, once spoken aloud, felt devastating. It implied that everything we were designing might rest on a false premise. It undermined our confidence in the project's relevance. Some team members advocated switching to a more conventional track — something with clearer benchmarks, more accessible expertise, fewer unknowns.

We nearly did it. We nearly walked away.

**中文：**

### 2.1 首年团队选择太空

我们是第一年参加 iGEM 的团队。当需要选择赛道时，选择太空赛道几乎是一种本能反应。我们的机构——深圳大学和深圳先进技术研究院——处于学术生物学和先进工程学的交汇点。空间研究、天体生物学、生命支持技术是机构学术讨论的一部分。选择太空赛道感觉很自然。雄心勃勃，是的。但很自然。

起初，兴奋是纯粹的。我们想象着细菌在轨道实验室中漂浮。我们想象着工程化的细胞为人类在地球之外生活和工作做出贡献。叙事很干净：合成生物学赋能太空探索。我们想成为这个故事的一部分。

### 2.2 隐藏的怀疑

但随着时间推，我们开始了文献综述和项目范围的真正工作，一种安静的不安降临了。我们探索了几十个潜在方向：

- **密闭循环栖息地中的资源回收**——一条被反复走过的路，已有许多先行者。
- **藻类-细菌共培养用于氧气和营养交换**——理论优雅，实际操作极难控制。
- **通过工程微生物生产太空食品**——一个至关重要的需求，但这方向感觉与我们的分子生物学优势脱节。
- **面向微重力的生物反应器优化**——这个方向不断浮现，但每次我们围绕它深入探讨，找到的问题总比答案多。

每一次头脑风暴会议都让我们少了一分确定。想法都很有趣，但有什么东西感觉不对。我们越努力推进，就越意识到：我们实际上并不理解空间生物加工中的*真正问题*是什么。我们在读论文，但并没有与人对话。我们在做假设。

### 2.3 几乎放弃

有那么一段时间——大约持续了两周——团队认真考虑了完全放弃太空赛道。怀疑凝结成了一个令人不安的问题，没有人愿意说出口：

*"我们从未真正踏上太空。空间生物反应器真的会像在地球上那样工作吗？"*

这个问题一旦被说出，感觉是毁灭性的。它暗示着我们正在设计的一切可能建立在一个错误的前提上。它动摇了我们对项目相关性的信心。一些团队成员建议切换到更常规的赛道——有更清晰的基准、更容易获取的专业知识、更少的未知数。

我们几乎这样做了。我们几乎离开了。

## 3. The Turnaround — Choosing Difficulty / 转折——选择艰难

**English:**

What changed everything was not a single insight or a Eureka moment. It was a mindset shift, encouraged by our instructors and advisors, that reframed the doubt itself as data.

Our instructors did not tell us "you're wrong" or "keep going." Instead, they asked us a question that reframed everything:

*"What exactly are you doubting?"*

We sat with that question. We realized we were not doubting the feasibility of synthetic biology in space — we were doubting something much more specific. We were doubting whether the *physical regime* inside a space bioreactor was sufficiently understood by anyone — including the researchers who had been building bioreactors for space for decades.

This was the pivot. The doubt was not a weakness. The doubt was the starting point.

The mindset crystallized around an internal reformulation of the project's core stance: difficulty is not an argument against doing the work — it is, often, what makes the work worth doing at all. The doubts we had been treating as warning signs were, in fact, signals that we had found a problem worth engaging with.

For friskoli, this meant:

> **"We choose to engineer for microgravity not because the problem is solved, but precisely because it isn't."**

The unknown is not an argument against exploration. It is the reason for it.

Once we accepted this, the path forward became clear. Our research question was not "can we build something for space?" — that was premature. The real question was: *"What is the specific, physical bottleneck that makes space bioprocessing different from terrestrial bioprocessing, and can biology — rather than engineering — address it?"*

**中文：**

改变一切的不是单一的洞见或灵光乍现的时刻。是一种思维转变——在导师与顾问的鼓励下，将怀疑本身重新框定为数据。

导师没有告诉我们"你们错了"或"继续前进"。相反，他们问了我们一个重新框定一切的问题：

*"你们究竟在怀疑什么？"*

我们坐下来面对这个问题。我们意识到，我们并没有怀疑合成生物学在太空中的可行性——我们在怀疑的是更具体的东西。我们在怀疑太空生物反应器内部的*物理状态*是否被任何人——包括那些几十年来一直在为太空建造生物反应器的研究者——充分理解过。

这就是转折点。怀疑不是弱点。怀疑是起点。

我们的思维凝聚在对项目核心立场的一次内部重新表述上：困难不是不做这件事的理由——它往往恰恰是这件事值得做的原因。我们一直当作警告信号来对待的那些怀疑，实际上是表明我们找到了一个值得投入的问题。

对于 friskoli 而言，这意味着：

> **"我们选择为微重力进行工程设计，不是因为问题已经解决，而恰恰是因为它尚未解决。"**

未知不是反对探索的论据，它是探索的理由。

一旦我们接受了这一点，前进的道路就清晰了。我们的研究问题不是"我们能为太空构建什么吗？"——这还太早。真正的问题是：*"是什么具体的物理瓶颈使空间生物加工与地球生物加工不同？生物学——而非工程学——能否解决它？"*

## 4. The Key Question — Does a Space Bioreactor Actually Work? / 核心问题——空间生物反应器真能工作吗？

**English:**

Our research question distilled to a deceptively simple form:

> **"Does a space bioreactor actually work like one on Earth?"**

The word "work" here deserves careful unpacking. A bioreactor "works" when its microbial population converts substrate into product at a rate and yield that justify the effort. On Earth, this depends on several interrelated factors:

1. **Nutrient distribution:** Gravity-driven convection and buoyancy continuously mix the medium, bringing fresh substrate to cells and removing waste products.
2. **Gas exchange:** Bubbles rise, oxygen dissolves at the air-liquid interface, and CO₂ escapes — all assisted by gravity.
3. **Thermal regulation:** Density-driven flows redistribute heat, preventing localized hot or cold spots.
4. **Mechanical mixing:** Stirrers, impellers, and shakers augment natural convection, ensuring homogeneity.

In microgravity, items 1 through 3 largely disappear. The remaining option — mechanical mixing — becomes the sole mechanism for maintaining a functional bioreactor. But mechanical mixing in space introduces its own cascade of problems: added payload mass, increased power consumption, complex fluid dynamics (surface tension dominates over gravity, creating unexpected phase distributions), and — most critically — maintenance requirements on hardware that cannot be easily serviced.

This is where the key insight emerged: **maybe the entire approach — trying to make a space bioreactor work like an Earth bioreactor — is mistaken.**

**中文：**

我们的研究问题凝结为一个看似简单的形式：

> **"空间生物反应器真的能像地球上那样工作吗？"**

"工作"这个词值得仔细拆解。当微生物群体以合理的速率和产率将底物转化为产物时，生物反应器才算"工作"。在地球上，这取决于几个相互关联的因素：

1. **营养分配：** 重力驱动的对流和浮力持续混合培养基，将新鲜底物带到细胞周围并移除废物。
2. **气体交换：** 气泡上升，氧气在气液界面溶解，CO₂逸出——全部由重力辅助。
3. **热调节：** 密度驱动的流动重新分布热量，防止局部过热或过冷。
4. **机械混合：** 搅拌器、叶轮和摇床增强自然对流，确保均匀性。

在微重力下，第 1 到第 3 项基本消失。剩下的选项——机械混合——成为维持生物反应器功能的唯一机制。但太空中的机械混合引入了自己的一系列问题：增加的载荷质量、更高的功耗、复杂的流体动力学（表面张力主导而非重力主导，产生意想不到的相分布），以及——最关键的是——对无法轻易维修的硬件的维护需求。

关键洞察就在于此：**也许整个方法——试图让空间生物反应器像地球上的生物反应器那样工作——本身就是错误的。**

## 5. The Stirring Critique — The Wrong Solution to the Wrong Problem / 搅拌批判——对错误问题的错误解答

**English:**

This is the intellectual core of the Human Practices reflection in friskoli — the insight that emerged from questioning our own assumptions and that we believe has significance beyond our project.

### 5.1 The Assumption We Inherited

The standard approach to microgravity bioreactors assumes that the goal is to reproduce the *uniform mixing* achieved by gravity and impellers on Earth. The problem is framed as: "How can we mix thoroughly without gravity?" The solutions offered are mechanical: rotating wall vessels, magnetic stirrers, perfusion systems, acoustic streaming. Each addresses the symptom (lack of mixing) without questioning the diagnosis.

### 5.2 Why Uniform Mixing Is the Wrong Goal

Here is the critical insight — and we present it as a hypothesis, not an established fact:

**In microgravity, the optimal steady-state for a biodegradation bioreactor is NOT uniform distribution but SUBSTRATE ENRICHMENT.**

Let us explain. In a bioreactor degrading solid cellulose waste, the rate-limiting step is not microbial metabolism — it is the encounter rate between enzyme-secreting bacteria and their solid substrate. On Earth, passive diffusion plus gravity-driven microcurrents ensures that a bacterium somewhere in the reactor will eventually encounter a cellulose fiber. In microgravity, the absence of these currents means that a bacterium situated more than a few millimeters from a cellulose particle has essentially no chance of encountering it on any biologically relevant timescale.

If you mix the reactor uniformly, you achieve a homogeneous distribution — bacteria are everywhere, cellulose fragments are everywhere — but at any given moment, the *local density* of bacteria at the cellulose surface (where degradation actually happens) is low. You have solved the distribution problem, but you have not solved the encounter problem.

Now consider the alternative: instead of distributing bacteria uniformly, what if you engineered bacteria to *find and accumulate* at cellulose surfaces? You would achieve the exact opposite of uniform mixing — you would achieve substrate enrichment — but the encounter rate at the degradation site would be maximized.

**Uniform mixing is the right solution for a different problem (maximizing homogeneity for product recovery). For solid-substrate degradation in microgravity, it may be precisely the wrong approach.**

### 5.3 Three Reasons Mechanical Stirring Is Problematic in Space

Beyond the conceptual mismatch, mechanical stirring in space has practical liabilities:

| Liability | Earth Impact | Space Impact |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Payload mass** | Stirrer + motor: negligible fraction of total | Every gram costs approximately $10,000 to low Earth orbit |
| **Maintenance** | Bearings replaced routinely | No spare parts; no technician available |
| **Fluid dynamics** | Gravity stabilizes the gas-liquid interface | Surface tension dominates; bubbles do not rise; unpredictable phase distributions |
| **Power consumption** | Mains power, unlimited | Limited solar/battery budget; power is a zero-sum resource |
| **Single-point failure** | Backup systems available | One failed stirrer = entire bioreactor offline |

Our conclusion: **stirring the bioreactor is the wrong solution to the wrong problem.** The right solution may be to build bioreactor functionality into the biology itself — engineering microbes to navigate to their substrate rather than waiting for the substrate to be delivered to them.

### 5.4 The friskoli Reframe

friskoli proposes a biological alternative that takes the previous conclusion seriously: do not try to recreate Earth-style mixing in microgravity. Instead, engineer bacteria to seek out their substrate by chemotaxis, and let degradative activity *concentrate* at the substrate surface rather than spreading uniformly through the reactor. The mechanical stirrer is not replaced by a biological one — it is removed from the design problem entirely. The function it used to provide (bringing cells and substrate together) is achieved by a fundamentally different mechanism: directed bacterial migration.

We do not claim this is proven. We claim it is worth testing. The Human Practices journey brought us here: from "how do we build a better bioreactor?" to "what should a bioreactor in space actually try to accomplish?"

**中文：**

这是 friskoli 人类实践反思的智识核心——这个见解源于对我们自身假设的质疑，我们相信它具有超越本项目的影响。

### 5.1 我们继承的假设

微重力生物反应器的标准方法假设，目标是在微重力下重现地球上的重力和叶轮所实现的*均匀混合*。问题被框定为："没有重力，我们如何彻底混合？"提供的解决方案都是机械性的：旋转壁容器、磁力搅拌器、灌注系统、声流。每一种都处理症状（缺乏混合）而未质疑诊断。

### 5.2 为什么均匀混合是错误的目标

这里是关键洞察——我们将其作为假说提出，而非既定事实：

**在微重力下，生物降解反应器的最优稳态不是均匀分布，而是底物富集。**

让我们解释。在一个降解固体纤维素废物的生物反应器中，限速步骤不是微生物代谢——而是分泌酶的细菌与其固体底物之间的接触率。在地球上，被动扩散加上重力驱动的微流确保了反应器中某处的细菌最终会遇到纤维素纤维。在微重力下，这些微流的缺失意味着距离纤维素颗粒超过数毫米的细菌在任何生物学相关时间尺度上几乎没有机会遇到底物。

如果你将反应器均匀混合，你实现了同质分布——细菌无处不在，纤维素碎片无处不在——但在任何给定时刻，纤维素表面（降解实际发生的地方）处的细菌*局部密度*很低。你解决了分布问题，但你没有解决接触问题。

现在考虑替代方案：与其均匀分布细菌，不如工程改造细菌使其*找到并聚集*在纤维素表面？你将实现与均匀混合恰好相反的结果——你将实现底物富集——但降解位点的接触率将被最大化。

**均匀混合是另一个问题（最大化同质性以进行产物回收）的正确解决方案。对于微重力下的固体底物降解，它可能恰恰是错误的方案。**

### 5.3 机械搅拌在太空中存在问题的三个理由

除了概念上的不匹配外，太空中的机械搅拌还有实际缺陷：

| 缺陷 | 地球影响 | 太空影响 |
|------|---------|---------|
| **载荷质量** | 搅拌器+电机：总质量的微小部分 | 每克到近地轨道成本约 $10,000 美元 |
| **维护** | 轴承常规更换 | 无备件；无技术人员可用 |
| **流体动力学** | 重力稳定气液界面 | 表面张力主导；气泡不上升；相分布不可预测 |
| **功耗** | 市电供电，无限制 | 有限的太阳能/电池预算；电力是零和资源 |
| **单点故障** | 有备用系统 | 一个搅拌器故障 = 整个生物反应器离线 |

我们的结论：**搅拌生物反应器是对错误问题的错误解答。** 正确的方案可能是在生物学本身中构建生物反应器功能——工程改造微生物使其主动寻找底物，而非等待底物被送到它们面前。

### 5.4 friskoli 的重新框定

friskoli 提出了一个将前面结论贯彻到底的生物学替代方案：不去尝试在微重力下重建地球式的搅拌，而是工程改造细菌使其通过趋化性主动寻找底物，让降解活性**集中**于底物表面，而非均匀散布于整个反应器。机械搅拌器没有被一个生物搅拌器取代——它被从设计问题中**彻底移除**。它原本提供的功能（让细胞与底物接触）由一种根本不同的机制实现：定向的细菌迁移。

我们并不声称这已被证明。我们声称这值得检验。人类实践的旅程将我们带到这里：从"我们如何建造更好的生物反应器？"到"太空中的生物反应器究竟应该试图实现什么？"

## 6. Expert Engagement — Learning from the Field / 专家参与——向领域学习

**English:**

### 6.1 Engagement Philosophy

Human Practices in iGEM is not about asking experts to validate what we already plan to do. It is about allowing expert perspectives to reshape the project — sometimes in uncomfortable ways. We approached our expert engagement with three principles:

1. **Listen before proposing.** We did not present friskoli as a finished idea and ask for approval. We described the problem as we understood it and asked: "What are we missing?"
2. **Seek disagreement.** We specifically sought out researchers whose work suggested different approaches from ours. Comfortable agreement does not improve a project.
3. **Document the impact.** Every conversation was recorded (with permission), transcribed, and discussed by the full team. Changes to the project design were explicitly traced back to specific feedback.

## 8. Ethics — The Responsibility of Engineering Life / 伦理——工程改造生命的责任

**English:**

### 8.1 Honest Framing of Module 1 and Module 2

friskoli's two engineering modules carry different levels of biological novelty, and we describe both honestly.

**Module 1 (chemotaxis & metabolism)** activates an endogenous silent gene — the *asc* operon — that already exists, intact and sequence-complete, in the *E. coli* MG1655 genome. Hall & Xu (1992) demonstrated that this operon is functional when its native AscG repression is relieved. Our engineering here amounts to replacing the native regulatory region with a synthetic promoter — a genetic "on switch." This is an intervention of **regulation, not innovation**: under strong selection (e.g., cellobiose as sole carbon source), *E. coli* can acquire mutations or regulatory changes that derepress β-glucoside operons such as *asc* (Parisutham 2015).

**Module 2 (surface display)** is more conventional synthetic biology: we introduce a heterologous outer-membrane anchor (INPNC from *Pseudomonas syringae*) fused to heterologous cellulases (Cel48S, Cel9K, Cel5L from *Clostridium thermocellum*). These proteins are well characterized and are not pathogenicity factors of their source organisms, but they are genuinely foreign to *E. coli*. We do not minimize this.

What we do *not* do is create capabilities that have never existed in any organism, or combine functions in ways that would generate novel pathogenicity, novel host range, or environmental advantages outside the controlled bioreactor. The combined system degrades a polysaccharide — something many bacteria already do — and uses chemotaxis to navigate toward it — also something many bacteria already do. The novelty is in the *coupling*, not in either function alone.

Beyond Module 1 and Module 2, friskoli has no engineered enhancement to stress response, DNA repair, cell-division regulation, or quorum sensing. In those domains, our strain behaves identically to wild-type MG1655 — we did not engineer them, and we do not claim improvements where we made none. This boundary is part of our honesty commitment: a synthetic biology project is credible to the extent that its claims match its interventions.

### 8.2 What We Do NOT Claim

In the interest of honest scientific communication, we explicitly state what friskoli is NOT:

- **friskoli is not a solution to world hunger.** Our engineered bacteria do not produce food. They may assist in waste processing in a specialized, confined environment (space bioreactors), but they are not designed for agricultural or nutritional applications.
- **friskoli is not a cure for disease.** No medical claims are made. Our chassis is a non-pathogenic laboratory strain of *E. coli*.
- **friskoli is not "saving the planet."** While cellulose degradation has environmental relevance, our system is designed for closed bioreactor environments, not for environmental release.
- **friskoli is not ready for space.** We are at the proof-of-concept stage in terrestrial laboratory conditions. Microgravity simulation (RPM) is a proxy, not the real environment. We use conditional language ("would," "could," "we expect") throughout to reflect this uncertainty.

### 8.3 Containment by Design

The engineered strain is contained through a combination of intrinsic strain properties and operational design:

- **Chassis:** *E. coli* MG1655 is a well-characterized, non-pathogenic K-12 derivative classified as Risk Group 1. It is prototrophic and can grow on minimal media in the laboratory, but decades of lab adaptation have left it with reduced environmental fitness — it is a weak competitor against wild environmental microbes outside controlled culture conditions.
- **No environmental release pathway:** Our system is designed explicitly for closed bioreactor operation. We do not envision environmental deployment, and we do not introduce any genetic feature (e.g., novel toxin or invasive trait) that would create a release risk.
- **Regulatory compliance:** All work is conducted at BSL-1 in accordance with institutional biosafety guidelines. See our Safety page (09-Safety.md) for details.
- **Planned engineered safeguards** *(future work, not in current design):* We are evaluating whether to add an engineered auxotrophy (e.g., ΔthyA, requiring exogenous thymidine) or an inducible kill-switch as a designed containment layer for any future space-relevant deployment. This is documented in our Proposed Implementation page (§10 Safety and Containment).

### 8.4 The Broader Ethical Question

Beyond the specific safety features of friskoli, we engaged with a broader ethical question: **What does it mean to engineer organisms for an environment — space — that we ourselves cannot survive in without artificial life support?**

This is a question about responsibility and humility. Space is not an environment that life evolved for. When we send engineered organisms into space, we are placing them in conditions fundamentally different from anything in their evolutionary history. The ethical obligation is not only to prevent harm (safety) but also to understand the context deeply enough to make responsible decisions about whether and how to intervene.

We do not have a definitive answer to this question. But we believe that asking it — and being transparent about our uncertainty — is an essential part of responsible synthetic biology practice.

friskoli 的两个工程模块在生物学新颖性上处于不同层级，我们对两者都做诚实说明。

**模块一（趋化与代谢）** 激活了大肠杆菌 MG1655 基因组中本就存在、序列完备的内源沉默基因——*asc* 操纵子。Hall & Xu（1992）证明该操纵子在 AscG 抑制被解除后是有功能的。我们的工程改造是用合成启动子替换天然调控区——一个遗传"开关"。这是一种**调控、而非创造**的干预：在强选择压力下（如纤维二糖为唯一碳源），大肠杆菌可通过获得突变或调控变化来解除 β-葡萄糖苷操纵子（如 *asc*）的抑制（Parisutham 2015）。

- **friskoli 不是治疗疾病的方法。** 不提出任何医学主张。我们的底盘是一种非致病性的大肠杆菌实验室菌株。
- **friskoli 不是"拯救地球"。** 尽管纤维素降解具有环境相关性，但我们的系统是为密闭生物反应器环境而设计的，而非环境释放。

- **底盘：** 大肠杆菌 MG1655 是一种特征明确、非致病性的 K-12 衍生株，归类为风险等级 1。它是**原养型**，在实验室中可在基本培养基上生长；但几十年的实验室适应使其在野生环境中的适应度下降——离开受控培养条件后，它在与野生环境微生物的竞争中处于弱势。
- **无环境释放途径：** 我们的系统明确设计用于密闭生物反应器运行。我们不设想环境部署，也不引入任何会带来释放风险的遗传特征（如新毒素或入侵性性状）。

### 8.4 更广泛的伦理问题

除了 friskoli 的具体安全功能外，我们还参与了一个更广泛的伦理问题：**将一个生物体工程改造为适用于一个环境——太空——而这个环境我们自己如果没有人工生命支持也无法在其中生存，这意味着什么？**

## 10. Societal Impact — A New Paradigm for Space Bioreactors / 社会影响——空间生物反应器的新范式

**English:**

### 10.1 Beyond the Current Paradigm

If validated through rigorous experimental testing, friskoli would offer a new paradigm for space bioreactor design. The current paradigm is:

> "Build a sturdy vessel, add mechanical mixing, and hope the biology adapts."

The friskoli paradigm would be:

> "Engineer the biology to navigate to its substrate, and design the vessel to support that biology."

This is a fundamentally different relationship between biology and engineering. In the traditional view, the bioreactor is a container, and biology happens passively inside it. In the friskoli view, the bioreactor is a collaboration — the vessel provides the boundary conditions, and the biology provides the active spatial targeting.

### 10.2 Implications for Space Mission Architecture

If the chemotactic-targeting approach proves effective in microgravity, it would have cascading implications for how we design space missions:

| Aspect | Mechanically Stirred Approach | friskoli Approach |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Mass budget** | Reactor + motor + power supply + spare parts | Reactor + bacteria (grams) |
| **Power budget** | Continuous mechanical power for stirring | Minimal beyond thermal regulation and sensors |
| **Reliability** | Mechanical components fail; single point of failure | Biological redundancy: millions of independent cells |
| **Scalability** | Limited by motor size and impeller geometry | Limited by vessel volume and substrate surface area |
| **Adaptability** | Fixed mixing pattern; cannot follow substrate | Cells respond to changing substrate distribution by chemotaxis |

### 10.3 The Universal Platform Concept

Cellobiose is the repeating unit of cellulose. The asc operon provides cellobiose chemotaxis. But the principle is generalizable: any PTS sugar transporter could theoretically be substituted, enabling chemotaxis toward different substrates. If a surface-displayed enzyme breaks down a different polymer — releasing a sugar that feeds into a different PTS transporter — the same positive feedback architecture could be applied to a different waste stream.

This concept — a **universal platform** for substrate-directed chemotaxis coupled to enzymatic degradation — could, in principle, address diverse waste streams. The constraint is that the chemotactic signal must come from a **PTS** sugar (so that transport flux modulates EII~P → CheA), which narrows the candidate list:

- **Cellulose → cellobiose → AscF (cellobiose PTS)** — the current friskoli design.
- **Chitin → N,N′-diacetylchitobiose / chitin oligosaccharides → ChbBCA (chitobiose/chitin-oligosaccharide PTS)** — *E. coli* encodes this PTS, but induction and downstream metabolism are nontrivial; this would be a plausible extension, not a direct plug-and-play replacement.
- **Other β-glucosides** such as arbutin and salicin may also be compatible with AscF-mediated PTS input, but each substrate would require separate validation of transport, metabolism, and ecological relevance.

Maltose (from starch) and xylose (from hemicellulose) are *not* transported by PTS in *E. coli*; the maltose system uses an ABC transporter (MalEFGK₂), and xylose uses a proton symporter (XylE) and an ABC transporter (XylFGH). Coupling these to chemotaxis would require either a different signaling mechanism or heterologous expression of a PTS-coupled maltose/xylose transporter from another organism — substantially more engineering than the cellobiose case.

We emphasize: these are long-term, conceptual possibilities. We are demonstrating the principle with cellulose because the *asc* operon provides a tractable, PTS-coupled starting point.

**中文：**

### 10.1 超越当前范式

如果通过严格的实验测试得到验证，friskoli 将为空间生物反应器设计提供一个新的范式。当前范式是：

> "建造一个坚固的容器，添加机械混合，并希望生物学能够适应。"

friskoli 的范式将是：

> "工程改造生物学使其主动寻找底物，并设计容器来支持这种生物学。"

这是一种生物学与工程学之间根本不同的关系。在传统观点中，生物反应器是一个容器，生物学被动地在其内部发生。在 friskoli 观点中，生物反应器是一种协作——容器提供边界条件，生物学提供主动的空间靶向。

### 10.2 对太空任务架构的影响

如果趋化靶向方法在微重力下被证明有效，它将对我们设计太空任务的方式产生级联影响：

| 方面 | 机械搅拌方法 | friskoli 方法 |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **质量预算** | 反应器 + 电机 + 电源 + 备件 | 反应器 + 细菌（克级） |
| **电力预算** | 持续的机械搅拌电力 | 除热调节与传感器外极少 |
| **可靠性** | 机械组件会失效；单点故障 | 生物冗余：数百万个独立细胞 |
| **可扩展性** | 受电机尺寸与叶轮几何限制 | 受容器体积与底物表面积限制 |
| **适应性** | 固定的混合模式；无法跟随底物 | 细胞通过趋化性响应底物分布的变化 |

### 10.3 通用平台概念

这个概念——一个用于底物导向趋化耦合酶促降解的**通用平台**——原则上可以应对多样化的废物流。约束是趋化信号必须来自**PTS**糖（这样转运通量才能通过 EII~P 调节 CheA），这缩窄了候选范围：

- **纤维素 → 纤维二糖 → AscF（纤维二糖 PTS）** — 当前 friskoli 设计。
- **几丁质 → N,N′-二乙酰壳二糖 / 几丁寡糖 → ChbBCA（壳二糖/几丁寡糖 PTS）**——大肠杆菌编码这一 PTS，但其诱导与下游代谢并不简单；这属于有潜力的扩展方向，而不是可直接替换的模块。
- **其他 β-葡萄糖苷**，如熊果苷和水杨苷，可能也兼容 AscF 介导的 PTS 输入；但每种底物仍需分别验证转运、代谢与应用场景相关性。

麦芽糖（来自淀粉）和木糖（来自半纤维素）在大肠杆菌中**不**通过 PTS 转运；麦芽糖系统使用 ABC 转运蛋白（MalEFGK₂），木糖使用质子同向转运蛋白（XylE）与 ABC 转运蛋白（XylFGH）。将它们与趋化耦合需要不同的信号机制，或异源表达来自其他生物的 PTS 耦合麦芽糖/木糖转运蛋白——比纤维二糖案例需要的工程量大得多。

我们强调：这些都是长期的、概念性的可能性。我们正在用纤维素来证明这一原理，因为 *asc* 操纵子提供了一个可操作的、PTS 耦合的起点。

## 14. Reflections — What We Learned About Doing Science / 反思——关于做科学的感悟

**English:**

The Human Practices process taught us something unexpected about the nature of scientific work.

As undergraduate students, we are trained to learn what is known. Exams, textbooks, and laboratory courses all reinforce a view of science as a body of established facts. The Human Practices journey challenged this view at its foundation. When we began asking experts about space bioreactors, we discovered that many of the questions we considered "basic" had no settled answers. The field is not a textbook — it is a frontier.

This realization was initially disorienting. It meant we could not simply "look up" the right design for a space bioreactor. But it was also liberating. It meant that our project, however small, was genuinely contributing to an open question. The doubts that nearly made us abandon the Space Track were not evidence of our incompetence — they were evidence that we had found a real gap in understanding.

The most important thing we learned:

> **The questions you are afraid to ask are often the ones that matter most.**

**中文：**

人类实践的过程教会了我们一些关于科学工作本质的不曾预料到的东西。

作为本科生，我们被训练去学习已知的东西。考试、教科书和实验课程都强化了一种将科学视为既定事实体系的观念。人类实践的旅程从根本上挑战了这种观点。当我们开始向专家询问有关空间生物反应器的问题时，我们发现许多我们认为是"基础知识"的问题并没有确定的答案。这个领域不是一本教科书——它是一道前沿。

这一认识最初令人迷失方向。它意味着我们不能简单地"查找"空间生物反应器的正确设计。但它也是解放性的。它意味着我们的项目，无论多么微不足道，都是在真正地为一个开放问题做出贡献。那些几乎使我们放弃太空赛道的怀疑，不是我们能力不足的证据——它们是我们找到了真正的认知空白的证据。

我们学到的最重要的一点是：

> **你害怕提出的问题往往是最重要的问题。**
